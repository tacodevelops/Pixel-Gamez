# Moto X3M 4: Winter

Moto X3M 4: Winter takes the series' motocross stunt formula onto icy roads, trading the usual dirt-track scenery for a snow-covered setting packed with 25 new levels. The core challenge stays familiar — clear each course as fast as possible without wiping out — but winter-specific hazards give this entry its own identity within the series.

## How to Play

Racing through each level comes down to trial and error: read the obstacles ahead, accelerate and brake with care, and refine your run with each attempt. Moving cautiously tends to pay off more than rushing blindly into a jump, since crashes cost far more time than a slightly slower, more controlled approach.

Landing backflips shaves 0.5 seconds off your time whenever pulled off cleanly, which adds up over a full run and often makes the difference in reaching a top score. If a run does go wrong, restarting is quick — either through the arrow icon in the top-left corner of the screen or by pressing the spacebar.

**Winter-Specific Obstacles**

This entry introduces hazards suited to its icy setting: breakable glass ramps, air blowers that can throw off your trajectory mid-air, and candy ropes woven into some of the more festive-looking sections. Trigger-based nitro boosters also make an appearance, giving your bike a burst of speed at key points along a track.

## Progression and Bikes

Completing tracks quickly earns stars, which can be spent in the bike store on new rides. Two cosmetic upgrades are available: a green-colored rider on a quad, and a tattooed Santa riding a moped.

## Controls

- **W / Up arrow** — Accelerate
- **S / Down arrow** — Brake
- **A / Left arrow** — Tilt left
- **D / Right arrow** — Tilt right
- **Spacebar** — Continue race

## Features

- 25 winter-themed tracks
- Unique hazards, including breakable glass and nitro boosters
- Unlockable bikes and riders purchased with earned stars
- A rock soundtrack running throughout

## Background

Moto X3M 4: Winter was developed by MadPuffers, the studio behind the entire Moto X3M series. It was originally released as a Flash game in December 2017, with an HTML5 version following in November 2018.

## Platform

Playable in the browser.
