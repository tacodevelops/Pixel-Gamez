# Tennis Physics

Tennis Physics is a fun, arcade-style puppet sports game featuring retro-pixel graphics and chaotic, physics-driven gameplay. Much like other games in the "Physics" series, it simplifies the sport of tennis into a one-button challenge where unpredictable movement and timing are key to victory.

## How to Play

The game is designed around a simple one-button control scheme, making it accessible yet challenging due to the wobbly nature of the characters. You can play solo against the CPU or compete head-to-head with a friend in local multiplayer mode.

**Setup**

- **Teams:** Each side controls two puppet-like tennis players.

- **Objective:** Be the first to reach a score of 5 points to win the match.

- **Gameplay:** Players jump and swing their rackets simultaneously with a single button press. The goal is to hit the ball over the net and into the opponent's court while managing the wobbly physics of your team.

**Mechanics**

- **One-Button Control:** A single key press triggers a jump and a swing.

- **Chaotic Physics:** The players move in unpredictable ways, often leading to funny moments and unexpected shots.

- **Local Multiplayer:** Supports two players on the same keyboard or device.

## Features

- Simple and addictive one-button gameplay.

- Retro-pixel art style.

- Fun and unpredictable physics-based character movement.

- Local 2-player support.

- Fast-paced matches that are perfect for quick play sessions.

## Release Info

Tennis Physics is part of the popular "Physics" sports genre often found on browser-based gaming platforms. It follows the design philosophy of games like *Soccer Physics* and *Basket Random*, focusing on comedic, physics-heavy interactions.

## Platforms

- **Web Browser:** Playable on desktop and mobile browsers via HTML5.

- **Mobile:** Supports touch controls for play on tablets and smartphones.

## Controls

- **Player 1:** W key

- **Player 2:** Up Arrow key

- **Mobile:** Tap the screen to control your players.
